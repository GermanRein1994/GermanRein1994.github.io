Project Summary

Note on Localization: The interactive embedded dashboards retain their original Spanish layout. However, all structural insights, key metrics, and technical considerations are fully translated into English below each visualization.

This analysis focuses on monitoring continuous manufacturing equipment using sensor data (Temperature, RPM, Vibration, Energy Consumption). Utilizing a dataset of 10,000 observations recorded at one-minute intervals, the objective was to design a tiered comprehensive control system: starting from a strategic view of Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE) for management, down to parametric control for process engineering.

1. Operational Efficiency Dashboard (Strategic Level)

Designed for quick managerial evaluation. It provides a visual representation of historical shift performance and the relationship between energy consumption and Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE).

Analytical Considerations:
  • Continuous Availability: Since this is a process where the speed (RPM) never drops to zero in the records, a 100% Operating Time is assumed. Consequently, OEE losses are driven exclusively by Performance and Quality drops.
  • "Sub-optimal" State: The equipment operates under strict optimal conditions only 6.7% of the time. This does not imply a machine stoppage, but rather that the process functions outside its maximum theoretical efficiency regime. Even so, the equipment operates at 96.8% of its maximum speed, with an average quality rate of 94.5%.
  • Target OEE: If a target OEE of 91.5% is defined, it is met or exceeded 50% of the days. No significant differences are observed between shifts, and overall daily yields fall within the [91.2%; 91.7%] range.
  • Cumulative Energy Consumption: Month-to-date energy consumption amounts to 15,000 kWh. There is no direct correlation observed between power consumption and the overall performance of the equipment.

2. Process Control (Tactical Level)

A tactical tool for plant floor supervision. The continuous timeline allows auditing whether the physical parameters operated within safe setpoints over the last hours.

Control Methodology:
  • Improvement Opportunity: Currently, either due to recording omissions or a genuine lack of failures, the dataset does not feature a column indicating malfunction events. Incorporating this variable would directly impact equipment availability tracking and enable anomaly detection through forecasting models.
  • "Sub-optimal" State: 93.3% of the time, the equipment works in sub-optimal conditions. Again, this does not imply downtime, but indicates the process drifts outside its most efficient theoretical parameters.
  • Statistical Process Control (SPC): Pinpoints the exact moments when RPM, temperature, and vibration variables remain within their optimal operating ranges through reference bands.

3. Relationship Between Variables

Based on the Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) used to identify the machine's parametric boundaries, it was observed that there is no direct linear correlation between the different variables. For example, an increase in speed (RPM) does not cause a proportional increase in temperature or vibrations; the variables fluctuate independently.

Instead, this dashboard evidences a strictly deterministic "mold" behavior, where the optimal operating condition is only achieved when all three variables coincide simultaneously inside their safety "box".

Discovered Operational Boundaries:
  • Temperature: Operation strictly restricted between 74°C and 76°C.
  • Speed: Safe range validated between 1475 and 1525 RPM.
  • Vibration: Failure threshold when exceeding 0.055 mm/s.

Note: These boundaries are the inputs that feed the invisible reference bands on the Tactical Control dashboard (Above).
